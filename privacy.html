Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

Visionary is a personal productivity tool built by a college student and currently in early development. This policy explains what data is collected, where it is stored, and how it is used.

What we collect

When you create an account, Visionary stores your email address or the profile details returned by Google sign-in. When you use the app, it stores your tasks, scheduling choices, reflections, and related productivity data.

Where it is stored

Your account and app data are stored using Supabase. Data sent between your browser and the backend is protected over HTTPS.

Passwords

Your password is not stored in readable form. Passwords are hashed using a one-way cryptographic process handled by the authentication provider. If you sign in with Google, Visionary does not create a password for you.

Who can access your data

Other users should not be able to view your data. Visionary is intended to use per-user database access rules so each account can only access its own records. Because the app is operated by a solo developer, the developer may still be able to access stored data at the infrastructure level.

Tracking and analytics

Visionary does not currently use advertising trackers. If product analytics, sharing features, or third-party AI processing are added in a meaningful way later, this policy should be updated before those changes take effect.

Your choices

You can stop using Visionary at any time. If you want your account and stored data removed, contact the developer and request deletion.
